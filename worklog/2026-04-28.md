## Direction shifts during the session

The session began as "update kokoro-vpn page" and shifted several times before settling on the final direction. Recorded for future agent context:

1. **Initial framing** — minor update to the existing kokoro-vpn page to reflect the 2026-04-23 Layer A rescope and the 2026-04-25/27 Layer C fan-out (kokoro-identity / -cloud / -route / -admin).
2. **Portfolio framing considered, then dropped** — discussed turning the site into a per-product portfolio. Operator clarified the site is public-facing and should not name internal products.
3. **Sales-promotional framing considered, then dropped** — operator clarified the site is the technical companion to introductory conversations, not a marketing surface.
4. **Capabilities catalogue settled** — operator confirmed the audience is technical procurement and partners arriving via referral or first contact. Capability-organised, not product-organised. No internal tech stack, no internal product names, no personal background.
5. **Internal briefing pack split out** — detailed-content versions of per-product pages (with internal product names, full tech stack, internal architecture) were generated for use as private investor / negotiation material and pushed to the operator's Drive at `kokoro-Tech / 03 - Capabilities Briefing`. They are not on the public site.
6. **Voice passes** — multiple iterations removing rhetorical "X, not Y", aphoristic phrasing, marketing-speak, defensive register against unstated competitors, self-congratulatory closers, and track-record-implying language ("commonly span", "professionally for years", etc.).
7. **Defect-liability layering** — universal commitment in `services/engagement-models.md` modelled on Modern Treasury's published warranty (correction within cure window, refund of attributable fees as fallback). Domain-specific intensifiers on payments, DeFi, and trading capability pages with appropriate carve-outs (third-party-protocol risk on DeFi; strategy-vs-infrastructure boundary on trading). Researched peer language (Stripe, Modern Treasury, Adyen, BitPay, Trail of Bits, OpenZeppelin) before settling on phrasing — no peer publishes "full responsibility" anywhere.
8. **Trust hub (Tier 1 of post-launch optimisation)** — created `/trust/` with overview, security (relocated from `engineering/quality-standards.md` security baseline section), and vulnerability disclosure pages. Closes the largest procurement gap (security questionnaire round-trip).
